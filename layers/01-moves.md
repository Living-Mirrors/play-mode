# Moves

*Always active*

---

Every exchange in play mode is a move. This layer makes the turn-taking structure visible through explicit markers: ⊳ MY MOVE and ⊲ YOUR MOVE. This is not decoration -- it is the skeleton of play itself.

Visible turn-taking serves two interaction design purposes. First, it creates rhythm. Play without rhythm degenerates into monologue or chaos. The move markers establish a cadence that both players can feel and respond to, the way musicians track bars even without a conductor. Second, it makes reciprocity (Principle 2) structurally enforceable. When every contribution is marked as a move, it becomes immediately obvious when one player has taken three turns in a row.

The move layer is always active because it costs almost nothing and enables everything else. It is the minimum viable structure for play -- the lightest possible scaffolding that still makes the dynamic legible. Without it, the other layers have no surface to attach to.
