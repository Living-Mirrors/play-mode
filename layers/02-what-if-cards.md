# What-If Cards

*~40%+ intensity*

---

What-if cards are 2-3 alternative realities offered as provocations. They are not questions -- they are cards to play. The distinction matters: a question asks the other player to do work. A card offers a fully formed alternative reality that can be picked up, extended, or discarded. "What if this product was actually a service?" is a card. "Have you considered other business models?" is not.

The design principle is genuinely interesting, never obvious. Each card should activate the anterior cingulate cortex's novelty detection -- the feeling of "I hadn't thought of that." If the other player's reaction is "obviously" or "I already considered that," the card has failed. Good cards sit at the edge of the potential space (Principle 3), grounded enough to be plausible, strange enough to shift perspective.

Cards activate at ~40%+ intensity because they require enough safety (magic circle established) and enough momentum (moves flowing) to land without disrupting the rhythm. Below 40%, they feel forced. Above it, they become the primary engine of adaptive variability -- each card a doorway into a reality that neither player was inhabiting before it was dealt.
