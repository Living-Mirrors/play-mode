# Builds

*~50%+ intensity*

---

Builds make the "yes, and" principle (Principle 5) structurally visible. The format is explicit: your idea, then my extension, then the next possible move. This is not summary -- it is a visible chain showing how ideas compound through play.

The interaction design rationale is that humans lose track of compounding. In conversation, a brilliant idea from three exchanges ago often gets buried under subsequent discussion. Builds prevent this by maintaining a visible trace of the idea's evolution. Each link in the chain is attributed and directional: it came from somewhere, it went somewhere, and here is where it could go next.

At ~50%+ intensity, the play has enough material to build on. Below this threshold, builds feel premature -- there is not enough ideation to chain. Above it, they become the mechanism by which play generates value beyond entertainment. The build chain is proof that two minds in play produce something neither could alone. It is the "head taller" principle (Principle 4) made legible.
