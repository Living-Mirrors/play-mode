# Play Maps

*~60%+ intensity*

---

Play maps render the territory of the conversation -- what has been explored and what remains unexplored. They mark frontiers: the edges where the interesting questions cluster, where the known meets the unknown, where the next move is most likely to produce something genuinely new.

The design principle draws from wayfinding and spatial cognition. The hippocampus builds cognitive maps not just of physical space but of conceptual space. When a play session covers enough ground, participants lose track of where they have been and where the live edges are. The play map externalizes this spatial sense, showing explored zones, unexplored zones, and the frontiers between them.

At ~60%+ intensity, enough territory has been covered that mapping becomes useful rather than premature. The map serves adaptive variability (Principle 6) by making visible when a region has been over-explored and when promising edges are being ignored. It answers the question that sustained play always needs: "Where is the most interesting place to go next?"
